    About
    Me

    My digital literacy includes the design of a webpage and a booking system for a molecular image lab, web maintenance and the management of its messaging board for a sexual education site, auto imaging compilation and analytics at a neuropathic pain lab and devising algorithmic excel models for research management and strategy planning.

    In recent years, I have worked in a diverse range of positions, including research grants management, policy and practice analysis and research strategy planning, across several schools and departments at the university, and have increased my ability to perform assigned duties effectively and collaboratively.

    With specialisation in medical anthropology, my research interests and experiences are interdisciplinary and engaged in studies of culture, queer, gender/sexuality, social movements, Sinophone, history, science and technology, philosophy and bioethics. I also have an extensive background in biomedical, neuroscience and human physiology research.
